🇳🇬 Nigeria Macroeconomic Analysis Dashboard (1999–2026)

Overview

This project analyzes Nigeria's macroeconomic performance between 1999 and 2026 using Python, SQL, Excel, and Power BI. The objective was to identify major economic trends, explain key macroeconomic challenges, and provide data-driven policy recommendations through an interactive dashboard.

The dashboard tells a complete story of Nigeria's economy by examining GDP growth, inflation, exchange rates, public debt, oil prices, unemployment, government revenue and expenditure, and other important economic indicators.

---

Business Problem

Nigeria has experienced periods of economic growth alongside persistent challenges such as:

- High inflation
- Currency depreciation
- Rising public debt
- Heavy dependence on oil revenue
- Fiscal deficits
- Unemployment

Decision-makers require a clear and interactive way to understand these trends and identify potential policy responses.

---

Objectives

- Analyze 27 years of Nigeria's macroeconomic performance.
- Identify major economic shocks and turning points.
- Compare economic performance across different administrations.
- Highlight relationships between key indicators.
- Recommend evidence-based policy actions.

---

Tools Used

- Python (Data Cleaning & Preparation)
- SQL (Data Exploration & Business Queries)
- Excel (Data Validation & Initial Processing)
- Power BI (Dashboard Design & Visualization)

---

Dashboard Structure

Page 1 — Executive Overview

Provides a high-level summary of Nigeria's economy using KPI cards, major economic indicators, and key findings.

Page 2 — GDP & Economic Growth

Examines GDP trends, economic growth, unemployment, and inflation while highlighting major growth drivers.

Page 3 — Exchange Rate & Currency Stability

Analyzes the depreciation of the Nigerian Naira, exchange rate reforms, and their relationship with inflation and GDP.

Page 4 — Debt & Fiscal Health

Evaluates government borrowing, public debt growth, budget deficits, revenue generation, and fiscal sustainability.

Page 5 — Inflation Dynamics

Explores inflation trends, purchasing power, interest rates, and the economic factors driving inflation.

Page 6 — Oil Dependency

Investigates the relationship between crude oil prices, government revenue, and Nigeria's dependence on the oil sector.

Page 7 — Labour Market

Analyzes unemployment trends and their relationship with GDP growth and broader macroeconomic performance.

Page 8 — Policy Recommendations

Summarizes major economic challenges and presents practical policy recommendations based on the findings.

Page 9 — Final Summary

Concludes the analysis with the key insights gathered across all dashboard pages.

---

Key Findings

- GDP increased significantly over the study period.
- Inflation remained one of Nigeria's most persistent macroeconomic challenges.
- The Nigerian Naira experienced substantial depreciation, particularly after major exchange-rate reforms.
- Public debt accelerated rapidly after 2015.
- Government expenditure frequently outpaced revenue, contributing to fiscal deficits.
- Heavy dependence on crude oil continues to expose the economy to external shocks.

---

Recommendations

- Diversify government revenue beyond crude oil.
- Improve fiscal discipline and reduce unnecessary borrowing.
- Strengthen agricultural and manufacturing sectors.
- Promote export-led industrialization.
- Improve exchange-rate stability.
- Encourage private sector investment and job creation.
